Detailed Methodology

Method

  • We have an (n+1) * (n) matrix, with all rows summing to 0 and the final row summing to n/2
  • For each game such that i beats j, matrix[i][i]++, matrix[j][j]++, matrix[i][j] += -4/3, matrix[j][i] += -3/4
  • Postseason games are weighted more heavily using a linear multiplier of 1/3
  • Margin-of-victory is accounted for using a hyperbolic tangent function, capping at 1/3
  • The system is solved using least squares, optionally with Tikhonov regularization for the 2020 season

Premises

  • Each team's rating represents a context-aware average of their opponents' ratings
  • Game outcomes can be weighted by factors such as blowout margin and postseason status
  • Regularization helps stabilize rankings in sparse or disconnected schedules
  • Without any modifiers, we assume, without correction, that a team who beats another will be 4/3 as good as the team they beat
  • The final ratings are scaled such that the average team is scored at approximately 0.5
