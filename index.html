CVI 2.0 (LOGs) RELEASED!

The second version of CVI has been released! This version better accounts for games against weak opponents and weights better for postseason games. There are vast structural changes in CVI, most notably, shifting the SVS scale instead of using a deliberate affine transformation.

NERDS WIN, NERDS WIN, AND NERDS WIN!

ATLANTA, COLUMBIA, BERKELEY - Georgia Tech (3-0, AP: NR, CVI: 16), Vanderbilt (3-0, AP: NR, CVI: 24), and Cal (3-0, AP: NR, CVI: 40) all recorded impressive wins over Clemson (1-2, AP: 12, CVI: 55), South Carolina (2-1, AP: 11, CVI: 64), and Minnesota (2-1, AP: NR, CVI: 57) today. GT's kicker Aidan Burr executed a flawless 55-yard career-long walk-off kick to seal the win for Jackets 24-21, and fans stormed the field and carried the goalposts into the President's mansion. Vanderbilt quarterback Diego Pavia threw for 177 yards and 2 touchdowns in a statement win over the Gamecocks 31-7 on the road. Cal true freshman quarterback Jaron-Keawe Sagapolutele threw for 279 yards and 3 touchdowns as the Golden Bears start 3-0 for only the third time since 2007. All three teams are undefeated going into conference play.

REED, AGGIES WIN INSTANT CLASSIC IN SOUTH BEND

SOUTH BEND - Texas A&M (3-0, AP: 16, CVI: 3) beat Notre Dame (0-2, AP: 8, CVI: 26) for their first ranked away win in 11 years, 41-40 in South Bend. The Aggies started off slowly after a blocked punt turned into a touchdown for the Irish, and took their first lead near the half. After a botched extra point by Notre Dame, quarterback Marcel Reed drove the Aggies down the field, capping off a 293-yard performance with a game-winning touchdown pass on 4th and goal from the 12. Notre Dame, last year's runner-up, has yet to win a game this season, and Texas A&M is undefeated going into conference play.

METHODOLOGY

The computer votecasting index (CVI) has each team submit "votes" for the teams it played, based on how well a team performed in relation to their average opponent. Votes are then used to shift the scale until convergence is achieved.
